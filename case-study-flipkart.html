01. Business Problem / Challenge

The client required a learning platform that could support both external and internal audiences, which posed a challenge when using a traditional LMS setup.

Public Enrollment

Needed public learner enrollment instead of admin-created users.

Multiple Personas

Managing multiple learner personas with different learning needs.

Custom Data

Capturing custom learner information during registration.

Automation

Automating training assignments to avoid manual administrative effort.

02. Solution & Implementation Approach

The implementation focused on automation, learner segmentation, and scalability, while working within the customization constraints of a standard LMS product.

1

Role-Based Learning Journeys

Defined distinct learner categories (external partners, sellers, internal teams), configured separate learning paths and content visibility, and ensured learners accessed only relevant, role-specific content.

2

Public Enrollment Workflow

Implemented a public registration form to allow self-enrollment and enabled an approval mechanism to maintain access control and data accuracy.

3

Customized Registration Experience

Introduced minor label and field-level customizations to capture unique learner data required for reporting and segmentation, balancing client needs with LMS product limitations.

4

Automated Training Assignment

Configured multiple automated workflows based on enrollment criteria to assign courses and curricula automatically upon approval, eliminating the need for ongoing admin intervention.

03. Results & Impact

All results shared at a high level to respect confidentiality.

Successful Launch

Successfully launched a public-facing LMS for external partners.

Streamlined Onboarding

Streamlined learner onboarding through automated workflows.

Scalability

Enabled rapid expansion across multiple brands and sellers.

Reduced Overlay

Reduced operational overhead for LMS administrators.

Growth Foundation

Created a scalable foundation for long-term program growth.

04. Key Learnings

  • Public enrollment significantly changes LMS architecture and workflows.
  • Automation is critical for managing high-volume external learners.
  • Even limited customization can deliver strong outcomes when designed thoughtfully.
  • Early definition of learner personas improves content relevance and adoption.
